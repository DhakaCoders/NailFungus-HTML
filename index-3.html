The first root cause

of toenail fungus is having cracked or brittle toenails.

Having brittle nails can create a breeding environment for different types of nail fungus.

And if you’re not sure if you have brittle nails…Here are a few questions to help you determine if you do. Do your nails split at the ends? Do they peel back at the tips? Have you developed ridging on your nails? Are they difficult to grow?

If the answer is yes to any of these questions…

Then you have brittle and unhealthy nails. And when your nails are brittle and unhealthy they have a tendency to crack, making it easier for the fungus to get in.

Once the fungus infiltrates your toenails…it’s then able to thrive due to the warm and moist environment.

So what causes your nails to become brittle? There are a variety of reasons.

For example…Problems with your Thyroid can weaken your nails and so can Psoriasis.

Poor circulation can also weaken your nails. But a weak or compromised immune system is the most common reason for brittle and weak nails.

And any one of these issues can put you at risk for ultimately developing toenail fungus.
