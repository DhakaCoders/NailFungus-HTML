My feet felt like they were suffocating so I did what my wife was terrified that I would do…

I took my sandals off.

At first, I just sat in my chair. Reveling in the fact that my feet were finally free. The fresh air and the feel of the warm pool decking felt wonderful.

And just as I was really beginning to relax and enjoy it…

My wife walked over to introduce me to her friend Carmen, and...

As she walked up I could see the blood drain from her face...

She could barely get the words out.Uh…Carmen…th…this is my hu…husband. It was too late for damage control, my zombie feet where there in all their glory,

So I quickly stood up trying to bring Carmen's eyes up and away from my feet.

But it was too late...It was as if her eyes were magnetic and my feet were made from solid iron. She couldn’t help herself. I quickly grabbed her hand and shook it.Trying to engage her in conversation...anything to take her mind off my grotesque feet.

I felt a terrible wave of shame rush over me. Of course, she was polite about it…But my wife?

Well…that’s another story.

She barely talked to me on the drive home. And when we crawled into bed, I made sure to put my socks on and keep my feet as FAR away from her as I could. I felt like a sad little puppy who had soiled the rug. So needing to be told I was loved, yet very aware of what I had done. But this wasn’t the first time. Truth be told my wife was repulsed by my toenails and we rarely got close.

When we did, I ALWAYS had to wear socks. It was humiliating. But that night something changed. I could feel it. And for the first time in our marriage I was worried we wouldn’t make it.

My wife of 12 years was disgusted by my feet.

That night, I lay awake for hours, staring at the ceiling and feeling my heart constrict in my chest, imagining life without my wife.

Was this it? Was I going to lose her over toenail fungus? And if she didn’t want me - would anyone else?

I tossed and turned all night thinking of how I could finally get rid of this toenail fungus. But what could I do about it?

I’d already spent well over three thousands dollars on treatments over the years. No matter how hard I tried, no matter how much money I wasted, no matter how many “specialists” I saw, nothing worked.

The way I saw it, I had three options.

I could ignore the thoughts and the fears swirling around in my head and watch what was left of my marriage slowly disintegrate as we headed for the divorce I knew was in our future if I didn't do something.

Two, I could get mad and bitter, tell her how shallow she was and play the victim.

Or three, I could fight for my wife, our family and my dignity.
